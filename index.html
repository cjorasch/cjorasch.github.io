Step 1

Enter your action items

At the end of your meeting, use the app to record all of your action items.

The system automatically looks at your calendar to know who is in the meeting. You can use simple phrases like "Tom, finish the budget by next Tuesday" and the system will figure out who to assign it to based on your calendar.

Step 2

Action Items are Created

The system creates action items that are assigned to people in your meeting with specific due dates.

Step 3

Notifications are Sent

Participants receive an email message that clearly identifies tasks that are to completed and due dates for those tasks. No longer do you need to worry about there being confusion about what needs to get done.

Step 4

Participants Mark Completed Tasks

Meeting participants can easily click on buttons within the email message to mark tasks when they have been completed. They don't even need to be signed up for the service in order to record their progress.

If they are using Meetings2 then they can also use the app or website to see lists of all their tasks across meetings and easily understand all the things they need to get done.

Step 5

Instantly see the status for all action items

Use the system to monitor the status of tasks at any time. All the information is in one place so you can see exactly where things stand.

Step 6

Let the system nag people for you

Tired of nagging people for status updates? The system will do this for you automatically. It's like having a project manager keeping track of the details for all of your meetings.

Step 7

Start the next meeting right

When you start the next meeting, everyone can see exactly where you are with all of the action items from the prior meeting. Save time by focusing only on items where there are issues vs. re-hashing everything that has been done.

People who are not being productive can not hide. Everyone in the meeting quickly sees who is being effective.
